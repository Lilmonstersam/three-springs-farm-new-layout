# Homepage Benefits Section: Audit + Consolidated Copy
**Page:** https://threespringsfarm.com.au/ | **Date:** 13 July 2026

---

## 1. Audit Findings

### Duplication
Three separate sections currently make overlapping benefit claims:

| Claim | "How Can You Benefit" | "Centuries of Health Benefits" | "Why Choose" |
|---|---|---|---|
| Anti-inflammatory / joint pain | Card 2 | Intro para + WHM bullet + CHM bullet | – |
| Antioxidant / free radicals | Card 3 | Intro para + WHM bullet | – |
| Immune support | Card 1 | Intro para + WHM bullet | – |
| Liver protectant / digestion | Card 2 | WHM bullet | – |
| Tonic / wellbeing | Card 1 | Intro para + CHM bullet | – |
| Potency / synergy of actives | – | – | Final para |

Joint pain, immune support and antioxidant claims each appear 3+ times in slightly different wording. This dilutes the page, splits keyword relevance across sections, and reads as padding.

### Inconsistencies
- Naming drift: "Traditional Western" vs "traditional Western herbal medicine" vs "Western Herbal Medicine"; "Traditional Chinese medicine" vs "Chinese Herbal Medicine".
- "Anti–Inflammatory" uses an en dash mid-word; elsewhere it is unhyphenated ("anti inflammatory" in a testimonial).
- Benefit cards talk about *turmeric* generically; nothing ties the benefits back to *Complete 3 Turmeric* or why the liquid full-spectrum format delivers them better. The product argument (freshness, full spectrum, synergy) sits stranded in "Why Choose" with no bridge to the benefits.
- Ayurvedic uses (cardiovascular, respiratory, mental endurance) appear only in the second section and never in the benefit cards, so the two lists do not match.

### Messaging gaps
- **No bioavailability claim in the benefits block.** "Liquid absorbs faster than capsules" is the strongest differentiator, but it only appears in the hero and an FAQ.
- **No CTA anywhere in the benefits content.** Users read four screens of benefits with no action prompt until "Where Can You Buy".
- **No AUST L / TGA trust signal** near the health claims (the FAQ implies TGA listing; if the product holds an AUST L number, surface it here).
- **No keyword targeting for high-intent queries** like "turmeric liquid extract benefits", "organic turmeric extract Australia", "turmeric for joint pain". Current H2s ("How Can You Benefit From Turmeric?", "Turmeric: Centuries of Health Benefits") target generic informational turmeric queries the page will never win against health publishers.

### Recommendation
Merge "How Can You Benefit" + "Centuries of Health Benefits" into one section, and pull the potency/synergy line from "Why Choose" into its intro. "Why Choose" then focuses purely on provenance (farm, organic, fresh root, nothing stripped out), removing its overlap too.

## 3. Recommended Layout / Structure

```
H2  How Can You Benefit From Complete 3 Turmeric™?
    Intro paragraph (centred, max ~65ch width)

    [4-card grid: 4-up desktop / 2x2 tablet / stacked mobile]
    Each card: icon + H3 (benefit-led, verb-first) + 2-line body

    Trust strip: 3 small inline icons (mortar & pestle / yin-yang
    or lotus / leaf) + ACO logo, single line of text

    CTA band: one-line usage prompt + primary button (Buy Now)
    + secondary button (Subscribe & Save)
```

- **Heading hierarchy:** one H2 for the section, H3 for each card. Removes the current duplicate-weight H2s competing for the same topic.
- **Card headlines lead with the outcome** (Relieves, Protects, Supports, Strengthens), not the mechanism. Front-loads scannable benefit language and target phrases ("joint aches", "liver health", "immune").
- **Icons:** joint/knee icon, shield/cell icon, stomach/leaf icon, sun/vitality icon. Keep the existing brand gold/turmeric palette.
- **Bullets:** none needed inside cards; two-line bodies scan better than bullet fragments at card width.

## 4. Design Improvement Notes

1. **Insert the CTA band directly after the benefit cards.** This is currently the biggest conversion gap: benefits content has no purchase path until two sections later.
2. **Add the ACO logo (and AUST L number if held) adjacent to the health claims**, not only in the footer area. Proximity of certification to claims materially lifts trust.
3. **Cut page depth.** Removing the "Centuries of Health Benefits" section shortens the page by roughly two viewports, pulling testimonials and purchase options higher.
4. **Testimonials:** flag for a separate review. "Diagnosed with Inflammatory Arthritis" and "instead of over the counter pain killers" are the kind of implied serious-disease and comparative claims the TGA advertising code restricts. Speculative until checked against the current code, but worth a compliance pass.
5. **Standardise the en dash out of "Anti–Inflammatory"** and unify system naming everywhere: "Western Herbal Medicine", "Traditional Chinese Medicine", "Ayurveda".
6. **Alt text:** current images use description-of-file alt text ("Three Springs Farm Turmeric Extract Bottle with Shadow"). Rewrite to keyword-relevant alts, e.g. "Complete 3 Turmeric liquid extract bottle, certified organic, made in Australia".
